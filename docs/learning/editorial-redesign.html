The editorial reading design system

Asterion’s macOS interface is a cover-led library with a quiet detail pane and a separate long-form reader. It uses real catalog art as the visual focus and reserves interface color for selection, progress, and the primary reading action.

Research that shaped the plan

  • Apple Books treats covers, curated shelves, and “pick up where you left off” as the core library hierarchy.
  • Apple Books on Mac exposes font, background, spacing, justification, and column controls for reading comfort.
  • Readwise Reader keeps reading customizable and keyboard-friendly while separating the library from the focused document surface.
  • Matter’s reading themes use lower-contrast paper and sepia palettes to reduce glare.

Implemented design plan

  1. Replace the dense novel list with responsive Featured, Continue Reading, and Trending shelves.
  2. Keep the sidebar stable and native to macOS while giving selection a restrained oxblood treatment.
  3. Reduce the detail pane to cover, title, essential metadata, one primary reading action, synopsis, and five recent chapters.
  4. Use a warm paper surface and a literary serif for long-form text; use the system sans serif for controls and metadata.
  5. Keep the reader in its own window with adjustable text size, spacing, and measure.
  6. Preserve real loading, empty, authentication, progress, and failure states.

System map

  • Theme.swift owns the paper, ink, oxblood, muted, border, and progress tokens.
  • SidebarView owns app-area navigation and signed-in identity.
  • EditorialCatalogView owns cover shelves, rankings, library grids, selection, and progress tiles.
  • NovelDetailView owns the selected story’s reading action, metadata, synopsis, and recent chapters.
  • ReaderView owns the distraction-free reading window and its per-user typography preferences.
  • AppModel owns shelf data, server progress, and the distinction between chapter metadata and full chapter content.
